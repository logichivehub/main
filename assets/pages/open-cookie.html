Cookie Policy

1. What Are Cookies?

By accessing the “Site or any of our online services, you consent to our sending one or more cookies—small text files containing unique identifier strings—to your device. We employ both session cookies, which expire when you close your browser, and persistent cookies, which remain on your device until they expire or are deleted. You can learn how to manage or disable cookies by consulting your browser’s “Help” documentation. Please be aware that blocking or deleting cookies may limit your ability to use certain features of Targ products and services. We may also use third-party cookies (for example, Google Analytics—see https://www.google.com/policies/privacy/partners for more details).

By accepting cookies, you authorize us to automatically collect certain technical information from your device through various means, including “web beacons” or “clear GIFs” (together with cookies, “cookies and similar technologies”). This information may include your IP address, device identifiers, browser and operating system details, the pages you view before and after visiting our Site, the content you interact with, and timestamps of your visits. We may also track your engagement with our emails (e.g., opens, clicks, forwards).

2. Cookies Used by Targ Hosting

We use several categories of cookies to deliver and improve our products and services:

Strictly Necessary Cookies: Essential to provide the services you request, such as enabling secure communication and preventing attacks. Blocking these cookies will prevent you from using our services.

Preference (Functionality) Cookies: Remember your choices (language, region, login details) and optimize load distribution, improving performance and response times.

Statistics (Performance) Cookies: Gather anonymous data on how you use our services (pages viewed, links clicked) to help us enhance user experience.

Marketing Cookies: Track your online activity to deliver relevant ads and limit the frequency of repeated advertisements.

4. Cookie Duration

Session cookies expire when you close your browser. Persistent cookies remain until they expire (typically between 30 minutes and two years) or until you delete them. You can manage or disable cookies via your browser settings (often under “Tools” or “Preferences”). Blocking cookies may impair certain site functions. For detailed guidance, see your browser’s help files or https://www.allaboutcookies.org.

5. “Do Not Track” Signals

Because there is no uniform industry standard for responding to “Do Not Track” signals, we do not change our data collection practices in response to such signals.

We may revise this Policy periodically. Updated versions will be posted on our Site and will include the date of the latest revision. We will use reasonable efforts to notify you of significant changes.
